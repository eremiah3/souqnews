PoS operators divided as new charges commence

by SouqNews -

A group of Point of Sales (PoS) operators in Nigeria recently staged protests against the increased service charges that took effect on Monday. The Association of Mobile Money and Bank Agents in Nigeria had announced the new service charges in June, claiming they were necessary to ensure the survival of PoS operators. The new service charge framework entails higher charges for both deposits and withdrawals across different transaction amounts. The Concerned POS Operators in Nigeria, representing a coalition of PoS operators and owners, strongly criticized the increase, deeming it outrageous and anti-people. They expressed concern that the higher charges would exacerbate the hardships faced by many Nigerians, particularly in light of the removal of subsidies and other challenging economic policies. Kayode Salako, the Chairman of the group, emphasized the need for fair practices by financial service providers and PoS operators. While acknowledging the impact of recent government policies on businesses, he urged the Association of Mobile Money and Bank Agents in Nigeria (AMMBAN) to advocate for preferences from the government to ease the burden on POS operators and the public. In response to the increased service charges proposed by AMMBAN, the Concerned POS Operators in Nigeria offered their own service charge structure, which they believe to be more reasonable. Their proposed service charge system applies varying charges based on transaction amounts, aiming to strike a balance between supporting their members' interests and alleviating the burden on the Nigerian populace during this period of economic hardship. Furthermore, the group called on the Central Bank of Nigeria to review its cashless policy and increase the withdrawal limit for PoS operators and mobile money agents through various channels, including ATMs. By doing so, they believe it would enable PoS operators to better serve the public's financial needs and navigate the challenges posed by the increased service charges. In conclusion, the coalition of POS operators and owners stands in solidarity with the Nigerian population, urging for fair practices, affordability, and increased support from relevant authorities to ease the burden on both operators and customers during these economically challenging times.
