How abandoning mining sector affects Nigerian economy

by SouqNews -

Prof. Linus Asuquo, the Director-General of the National Metallurgical Development Centre (NMDC), has emphasized that the mineral and mining sector in Nigeria still remains largely underdeveloped despite its significant growth potential. The Ministry of Mines and Steel Development, in line with the Federal Government's policy thrust, believes that this industry holds the key to rescuing the country's economy. To achieve this, the administration's plan focuses on developing a private sector-led mining industry, with the government's role limited to that of a regulator enacting implementable legislations. By doing so, the sector aims to create much-needed job opportunities, a crucial aspect neglected in the underdeveloped state of the mining industry. Nigeria is rich in over 44 solid minerals, but the underdevelopment of these resources has contributed to a high unemployment rate, reaching 33.3 percent (23.2 million people) earlier this year, the highest in 13 years and the second highest globally. Despite vast deposits of valuable minerals like barytes, iron ore, copper, manganese, coal, limestone, bitumen, gold, lead-zinc, and graphite, the country has not been able to tap into their revenue potential effectively. Comparing Nigeria's mining contribution to other African nations, the figures show that Nigeria lags significantly behind, with only 0.3 percent contribution to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP), while other countries like the Democratic Republic of Congo, Botswana, and Zambia have much higher contributions. Prof. Linus Asuquo believes that reviving the Ajaokuta Steel Company presents the best option for the country. The construction of this steel complex has already consumed a considerable amount of funds, and abandoning it would be a mistake. He urges the government not to overlook the importance of the Ajaokuta Steel Company. In conclusion, adequate funding and attention to the mining sector could lead to significant development in Nigeria, creating job opportunities and contributing to economic growth. The revival of the Ajaokuta Steel Company, with its potential for job creation and power generation, coupled with the availability of iron ore for steel production nationwide, holds promise for the country's future.
